## Unit System Overview

Dawn of Gunpowder features a diverse roster of military units representing the warfare of the 13th century, a time when traditional medieval combat began to mix with early experimental gunpowder technology.

Units are organized into several battlefield roles. Each role fills a different tactical purpose and performs best when used in combination with others.

### Core Unit Roles

**Infantry**
The backbone of most armies. Infantry units hold the line, protect ranged units, and fight in sustained melee combat.

**Shield Infantry**
Heavily defensive soldiers equipped with shields and armor. They are designed to absorb damage and maintain the frontline during large engagements.

**Spearmen and Pike Units**
Polearm troops specialize in countering large enemies and cavalry charges. They are effective defensive units and often anchor the center of formations.

**Two-Handed Infantry**
Aggressive melee fighters wielding large weapons. These troops sacrifice defense for powerful attacks capable of breaking enemy lines.

**Archers**
Traditional ranged units that provide long-distance support. They are effective against lightly armored targets but must be protected from melee attackers.

**Crossbow Units**
More powerful ranged specialists capable of penetrating heavier armor. They generally fire slower than archers but deliver more devastating shots.

**Skirmishers**
Light ranged troops using thrown weapons such as javelins or slings. They excel at harassment, mobility, and disrupting enemy formations.

**Cavalry**
Fast-moving mounted units used for flanking, pursuit, and shock charges. Cavalry can quickly exploit gaps in enemy lines.

**Horse Archers**
Highly mobile ranged cavalry capable of firing while moving. These units are ideal for harassment tactics and hit-and-run warfare.

**Gunpowder Units**
Experimental early gunpowder troops including fire lances, primitive hand cannons, rockets, and explosive weapons. These units represent the early transition toward gunpowder warfare.

**Special Units**
Rare battlefield units such as war elephants or mounted artillery
